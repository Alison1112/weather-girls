ENERGY SUSTAINABILITY
To ensure energy sources are sustainable, renewable energy sources must be included. Renewable energy is generated from natural resources such as the sun, wind, biomass, hydrogen and water, using technology which ensures that the energy stores are naturally replenished.

CURRENT SITUATION
Offshore and onshore wind is the biggest source of renewable energy in the UK. It not only beats the other renewable sources by 13.8% of total electricity generation in the UK, it also beats the combined generation of coal, oil, and others by 6.5%.

REDUCE, REUSE, RECYCLE
Buying and producing products with minimal packaging (including the economy size when that makes sense for you) will help to reduce waste. And whenever you can, recycle paper, plastic, newspaper, glass and aluminum cans.
REDUCE GHG
GHG means Greenhouse gases. Scientists believe that greenhouse gasses are the culprits behind global warming... Unlike fossil fuels, renewable sources of energy like hydropower, wind and solar do not directly emit greenhouse gases, and life cycle assessments show that most sources are zero carbon.
ENERGY EFFICENCY
The road towards changing the impact of global warming includes daily decisions within your reach – like driving and flying less, switching to a ‘green’ energy provider and changing what you eat and buy.

  • Efficient use of Energy can combat Climate Change and Global Warming


    In a recent article on energy efficiency, Researchers at Lund University point out the need to move from a supplier based energy efficiency targeting stratergy to a demand-side approach. They are of the opinion that improved energy efficiency is significantly impacted by consumer behaviours

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions at Record High, Actions Required Now


    Greenhouse gas emissions driving climate changes have reached highs not seen in at least 3 million years, prompting UN scientists to warn that the “window of opportunity” to tackle emissions is rapidly closing. An initial course of action would be to curtails products and services that release CFC especially refrigeration, artificial cultivation of bacteria that consume GHG and storing more carbon in forests and soils via tree planting schemes

ADAPTATION TO CHANGE
The UK government published its updated strategy for tackling the impacts of climate change, including higher temperatures, drought and flooding. Adaptation aims to reduce vulnerability to the impacts of climate change that are inevitable due to past and current greenhouse gas emissions.
CLEAN TRANSPORT
In the United Kingdom, the transport sector accounted for the largest share of final consumption of energy at 40 per cent in 2016, the same share as in 2015, with the domestic sector accounting for 29 per cent, industry 17 per cent, and the services sector 14 per cent. (UK Gov, 2017)
